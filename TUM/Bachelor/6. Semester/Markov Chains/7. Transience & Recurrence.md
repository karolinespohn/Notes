# Definitions
- Let $(X_n)$ be a Markov Chain with the transition matrix $\Pi$ and the state space $E$
- Define $T_i:= \inf\{n >0 : X_n=i\}$
- $i \in E$ is **recurrent** if $\Pr^i[T_i<\infty] = 1$
- $i \in E$ is **transient** if $\Pr^i[T_i<\infty] < 1$
## Positive & Null Recurrence
 - A recurrent state $i$ is **positive recurrent**, if the expected value of the return time has a finite expectation: $E^i[T_i]< \infty$
 - It is called **null recurrent** if it is recurrent, but $E^i[T_i] = \infty$
## Condition for Recurrence
- A MC is recurrent iff: 
$$\sum_{n=0}^\infty\Pi^n(i,i)= \infty$$
## Communication & Recurrence
- Two states $i, j\in E$ with $i \leftrightarrow j$ are either both recurrent or transient
## Invariant Measures
- A measure $\alpha$ is called an **invariant measure**, if...
	- ... $\alpha(i) \in [0, \infty) \quad \forall i \in E$  
	- ...$\alpha \neq 0$
	- ...$\sum_{j \in E} \alpha(j) \Pi(j, i) = \alpha(i)$
